Theses

Flexible Monte Carlo methods for fluid and light transport simulations

PDF eScholarship
  • Supervisor: Prof. Derek Nowrouzezahrai
  • Institution: McGill University
  • Year: 2024
Summary: This thesis develops flexible Monte Carlo techniques for physically-based rendering and fluid simulations. First, we present a versatile two-stage mutation strategy based on the delayed rejection Markov chain Monte Carlo framework to generalize the Metropolis light transport algorithm. Then, we propose a Monte Carlo approach for fluid simulations using the Feynman-Kac stochastic representation of the vorticity transport equation and devise a recursive Monte Carlo estimator of the Biot-Savart law. We expand this method with a stream function formulation that enables us to manage free-slip boundary conditions using a Walk-on-Spheres algorithm.
