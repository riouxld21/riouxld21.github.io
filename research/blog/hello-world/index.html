After years of sharing research through papers and conference talks, I'm starting this blog as a more informal space for technical writing. The goal is to have somewhere to post the kinds of things that don't quite fit into a paper but are still worth writing down.

What to expect

I plan to write about a few different things here:

  • Technical notes on topics I'm actively working on — Monte Carlo rendering, importance sampling, real-time global illumination, and fluid simulation.
  • Reading notes from papers I find interesting, especially work at the intersection of rendering, MCMC methods, and physically based simulation.
  • Project updates on tools and experiments that come out of my day-to-day research at Ubisoft La Forge.
  • Retrospectives on published work — the ideas that didn't make it into the final paper, failed experiments, and lessons learned.

Why now?

I've been doing research in computer graphics for several years now, moving from computational fluid dynamics during my master's at the University of Montreal to light transport simulation during my PhD at McGill. Along the way I've accumulated a lot of notes, half-written explanations, and small experiments that never had a natural home.

This blog is that home. I recently rebuilt this website from scratch, which felt like a good excuse to finally add a blog section.

What I'm working on

My current research interests sit at the intersection of Monte Carlo methods and real-time rendering. I'm particularly interested in:

  • Reservoir-based importance sampling (ReSTIR and its variants) for real-time direct and indirect lighting
  • Markov chain Monte Carlo methods for light transport, including connections to path guiding
  • Denoising techniques that work well with low sample counts
  • Vortex methods and flow maps for fluid simulation

If any of that sounds interesting, stay tuned. More posts coming soon.
